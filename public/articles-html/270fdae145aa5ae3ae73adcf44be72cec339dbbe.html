‘Oh my gosh how long’s it going to be? He’s stopped breathing’: Family of murdered Australian baseballer listen to heartbreaking final moments of his life in court

  • Chris Lane, 22, from Melbourne, was gunned down on August 16, 2013
  • Family and friends broke down as they listened to an emergency call 
  • They heard details about how two bystanders tried to resuscitate Mr Lane
  • His mother Donna, walked out of court in tears after graphic testimony
  • Chancey Luna has been charged with Lane's murder
  • The trial is being held at Duncan District Court

By Cindy Tran for Daily Mail Australia and Aap

Published: 20:22 EST, 15 April 2015 | Updated: 03:29 EST, 16 April 2015

59 shares

The family of murdered Australian baseball player Chris Lane broke down in court as they listened to an emergency call revealing the distressing final moments of his life.

The 22-year-old, from Melbourne, was jogging along a street in the rural southern Oklahoma city of Duncan in August 2013, when he was shot in the back with a .22 calibre handgun.

His parents, Donna and Peter Lane listened to the frantic seven-minute 911 emergency phone call made by Oklahoma resident who witnessed their son collapsed on the side of the road.

Chancey Luna, 17, was charged with first-degree murder. The teen was riding in a Ford Focus with two teenage friends when he allegedly pulled the trigger as the vehicle drove past.

THE 911 EMERGENCY CALL 

Joy Smith: He's turning blue.

9-11 dispatch: Is he talking to you right now?

Joy Smith: No he's [gasping noise], that's all he's doing, making that noise.

9-11: All right, you just stay on the line with me, OK?

Joy Smith: If you don't hurry, he's gone.

9-11: Ma'am, they're coming OK, I can't make them come any faster.

Joy Smith: Oh my gosh how long's it going to be? He's stopped breathing.

Mr Lane, who was on a baseball scholarship with Oklahoma's East Central University, was visiting his girlfriend in Duncan when he was randomly gunned down.

Family and friends, including Mr Lane's former girlfriend Sarah Harper, heard details about how two bystanders tried to resuscitate the young man who had stopped breathing and wept during the proceedings at the Duncan's District Court on Wednesday.

Members of the family broke into loud, uncontrolled sobs when authorities showed the white baseball cap Lane wore when he was shot and photographs of the bullet wound taken in a hospital morgue.

Linda Prior cried as she told court how she attempted to shut Lane's 'big brown eyes' but wasn't able to after failing to resuscitate him.

'The big brown eyes were just staring at me,' Ms Prior, who has first aid training and performed CPR, told the court.

The 911 call after Christopher Lane was found shot in the back

The parents of slain Australian baseballer Chris Lane, Donna (right) and Peter, leaving Duncan District Court

Duncan woman Joy Smith (right) leaves court after she told court how she saw Mr Lane stumble and fall on the road. Smith's 911 phone call was played in court

Chancey Luna has been charged with first-degree murder after allegedly shooting Mr Lane in the back

Luna, a Duncan high school student, showed no emotion during the 911 recording and Ms Prior's evidence. 

Another local, Richard Rhoades, who also tried to help Lane, said he took off his shirt to place it over Lane's face when they realised he was dead. 

'I just thought it would be nice to close his eyes,' he said.

Luna's lawyer, Jim Berry, made a shock admission in court on Tuesday that his client had fired the bullet that killed Lane but added the teen was 'reckless' and was only trying to 'scare the jogger'.

The lawyer told the jury that for Luna to be found guilty of first degree murder, the prosecution would have to prove beyond reasonable doubt the murder was premeditated.

Prosecutors said Luna internationally pulled the trigger.

The trial continues.
